1. Introduction to the Solar System

Our Solar System formed about 4.6 billion years ago and continues to be a subject of great interest due to its complexity and the possibility of discovering life beyond Earth.

2. Planets of the Solar System

The Solar System consists of 8 planets that orbit the Sun

  • Mercury: The smallest planet and closest to the Sun. No atmosphere, extreme temperatures.
  • Venus: Thick, toxic atmosphere. Hottest planet due to greenhouse effect.
  • Earth: Our home, the only planet known to support life. Has breathable atmosphere and liquid water.
  • Mars: The Red Planet, known for iron oxide (rust) on its surface.
  • Jupiter: Largest planet with massive storms and many moons.
  • Saturn: Famous for its rings made of ice and rock.
  • Uranus: Rotates on its side; pale blue due to methane gas.
  • Neptune: Most distant planet; strongest winds in the Solar System.
